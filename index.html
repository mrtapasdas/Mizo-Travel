Where to go in Mizoram

Nine places, nine different reasons to come back.

A working shortlist — not everything in Mizoram, just the routes we'd send a good friend on.

Mizoram's highest peak, named for the haze that hangs over it most mornings.

The capital, built straight up a ridge. Sunset from Solomon's Temple, market mornings at Bara Bazar.

The state's tallest waterfall, dropping in three tiers through a forest gorge near Thenzawl.

A half-day trek from Aizawl into a recreated traditional village, with views over the Sairang valley.

Half-day trek

Murlen National Park

Remote rainforest near the Myanmar border, rich in birdlife and rarely visited by outsiders.

Champhai

Mizoram's handloom capital, perched on a ridge with valley views and artisans still weaving Puan on backstrap looms.

Weaving & valley views

Living culture

The hills come with a calendar of their own.

Spring opens with Chapchar Kut, a festival born from forest-clearing days — now a state-wide celebration of dance, food and rice beer. Dancers perform the Cheraw, stepping between clapping bamboo poles in rhythm so old nobody can date it. Weavers still hand-loom Puan, the striped cloth in our own logo, on the same backstrap looms their grandmothers used.

"Tlawmngaihna isn't a tourism slogan here — it's the reason a stranger gets the better half of the meal."

Chapchar Kut

Early March, statewide. The biggest festival on the Mizo calendar.

Puan weaving

Backstrap-loomed textiles, each pattern tied to a specific clan or occasion.

Mizo kitchen

Bai (boiled greens) and sawhchiar (rice-meat porridge) — comfort food, smoke-cooked.
